\begin{abstract}
We study unitary information spreading in the transverse-field Ising model
(TFIM) in its gapped ferromagnetic phase ($J=1$, $h=0.5$) on a small chain
($N=8$) using exact diagonalization. Starting from a simple product state,
we compute the entanglement growth of a half-chain and the two-site mutual
information $I(i{:}j,t)$ as a function of time. The resulting ``information
light-cones'' provide a concrete illustration of the IGD concepts of
informational locality (A9) and information velocity $v_{\mathrm{IGD}}$.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Setup}

We consider the TFIM Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
  H = - J \sum_i \sigma^z_i \sigma^z_{i+1} - h \sum_i \sigma^x_i
\end{equation}
with $J=1$, $h=0.5$ and open boundary conditions for a chain of $N=8$ sites.
We perform exact diagonalization, obtaining eigenvalues $E_k$ and eigenvectors
$|E_k\rangle$. As an initial state we choose the $\sigma^z$-polarized product
state
\begin{equation}
  |\psi_0\rangle = |\uparrow\uparrow\cdots\uparrow\rangle_z,
\end{equation}
which is not an eigenstate of $H$. The time evolution is
\begin{equation}
  |\psi(t)\rangle = e^{-iHt} |\psi_0\rangle = \sum_k c_k e^{-iE_k t} |E_k\rangle,
\end{equation}
where $c_k = \langle E_k|\psi_0\rangle$.

For each time $t$ on a grid $t\in[0,t_\mathrm{max}]$ we compute:

\begin{itemize}
  \item the reduced density matrix of the first half-chain and its entropy
        $S_A(t)$ (in bits);
  \item the two-site mutual information $I(i{:}j,t)$ and its average
        $I(d,t)$ over pairs with fixed distance $d=|i-j|$.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Results}

Figure~\ref{fig:half_entropy} shows the growth of half-chain entanglement
entropy $S_A(t)$ (in bits). Starting from zero at $t=0$, $S_A(t)$ increases
approximately linearly at early times and then bends towards a saturation
plateau set by the finite system size.

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{TFIM_Tevo_HalfEntropy_N8.png}
  \caption{Half-chain entanglement entropy $S_A(t)$ (in bits) for the
  TFIM with $N=8$, $J=1$, $h=0.5$, starting from the product state
  $|\uparrow\cdots\uparrow\rangle_z$.}
  \label{fig:half_entropy}
\end{figure}

Figure~\ref{fig:mi_heatmap} displays a heat map of $I(d,t)$ (in bits) as a
function of time $t$ and distance $d$. One clearly observes an information
light-cone: for each fixed distance, $I(d,t)$ remains close to zero up to a
characteristic time $t \sim d/v_\mathrm{eff}$, then increases and
eventually oscillates and saturates due to finite-size effects.

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{TFIM_Tevo_MI_Heatmap_N8.png}
  \caption{Heat map of the average two-site mutual information $I(d,t)$
  (in bits) vs time $t$ and lattice distance $d$ for the TFIM with $N=8$,
  $J=1$, $h=0.5$, starting from the product state
  $|\uparrow\cdots\uparrow\rangle_z$. An information light-cone structure
  is visible.}
  \label{fig:mi_heatmap}
\end{figure}

The apparent front is compatible with an effective information velocity
$v_\mathrm{eff}$ of order unity, consistent with the analytic IGD prediction
$v_\mathrm{IGD} = v_\mathrm{max} = 1$ for this parameter regime. A more
precise determination of $v_\mathrm{eff}$ requires larger systems and
matrix-product-state methods, but the qualitative behaviour is already clear
in this small-scale demonstration.

\section*{IGD interpretation}

Together with the static study of $I(d)$ in the ground state, these results
provide a fully explicit IGD example:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Informational locality (A9) appears as exponential decay of
        correlations with a finite $\xi = 1/\ln 2$.
  \item A finite information velocity $v_\mathrm{IGD}$ controls the spread
        of correlations and mutual information, giving rise to an emergent
        light-cone structure.
  \item The half-chain entropy $S_A(t)$ measures how the IGD trajectory
        explores more entangled regions of the focus manifold.
\end{itemize}
