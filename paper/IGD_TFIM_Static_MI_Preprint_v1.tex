\begin{abstract}
We compute the two-site mutual information $I(i{:}j)$ in the ground state
of the transverse-field Ising model (TFIM) in its gapped ferromagnetic phase
($J=1$, $h=0.5$) for a finite chain of length $N=8$. The decay of the average
$I(d)$ with lattice distance $d=|i-j|$ illustrates informational locality
in the sense of the IGD axiom A9 and is consistent with an exponential
correlation length $\xi = 1/\ln 2 \approx 1.443$ obtained
from the exact solution.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Model and analytic expectations}

We consider the TFIM with open boundary conditions
\begin{equation}
  H = - J \sum_i \sigma^z_i \sigma^z_{i+1} - h \sum_i \sigma^x_i,
\end{equation}
and fix $J=1$, $h=0.5$ (gapped ferromagnetic phase $J>h$). The exact dispersion
relation
\begin{equation}
  \epsilon_k = 2\sqrt{J^2 + h^2 - 2Jh\cos k}
\end{equation}
implies a spectral gap $\Delta = 2|J-h| = 1$ and a correlation length
\begin{equation}
  \xi^{-1} = \ln(J/h) = \ln 2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  \xi = 1/\ln 2 \approx 1.443.
\end{equation}

\section*{Numerical method and results}

We diagonalize $H$ exactly for $N=8$ and obtain the ground state $|\psi_0\rangle$.
For each pair of sites $(i,j)$ we compute reduced density matrices
$\rho_i$, $\rho_j$, $\rho_{ij}$ and the two-site mutual information
\begin{equation}
  I(i{:}j) = S(\rho_i) + S(\rho_j) - S(\rho_{ij}),
\end{equation}
where $S(\rho)=-\mathrm{Tr}(\rho\log\rho)$ is the von Neumann entropy.
We then average over all pairs with the same distance $d=|i-j|$ to obtain
$I(d)$, expressed in bits ($I / \ln 2$).

Table~\ref{tab:Id} lists the resulting $I(d)$ for the TFIM ground state
with $N=8$.

A semi-log plot of $I(d)$ vs $d$ shows a clear decrease with distance,
in line with the expectation of exponential decay. For such a small system
size the extraction of a precise numerical correlation length is limited by
finite-size effects, but the qualitative pattern already realizes
informational locality in the IGD sense.
